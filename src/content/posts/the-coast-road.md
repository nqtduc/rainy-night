I rented the bike at sunrise and didn't decide where I was going until the road
forked. Left went inland. Right hugged the sea. I went right.

The wind smelled like salt and diesel and something frying at a roadside stall.
I stopped twice — once for coffee, once just to sit on a wall and look at the
water for a long time.

These are the days I travel for. Not the famous places. The empty stretch of
road between them.
